Plant Care

Most plants need less from you than you think. In Malaysia's warm, humid climate, the usual killers are overwatering and dark corners — not neglect. A few habits cover almost everything we sell.

Light before everything

Bright, indirect light near a window suits nearly all our plants. If a spot is bright enough to read in comfortably during the day, it is bright enough for most greens. Avoid harsh afternoon sun through glass — it cooks leaves.

Water by feel, not by calendar

For potted and soil-free plants, touch the top of the medium: water only when it feels dry. When you do water, water thoroughly and let the excess drain. A plant sitting in a puddle is a plant in trouble.

Hydroponic jars

Refresh the water about once a week and keep roots two-thirds submerged — the exposed portion breathes. Rinse the jar and pebbles monthly. Roots should look pale and firm; trim any that turn brown and soft.

Air plants

Mist two to three times a week, or dunk in water for 20 minutes weekly, then shake off and let them dry upside-down before returning to their pot. Good airflow matters more than frequent watering.

Bonsai & miniature landscapes

Keep the moss lightly moist and give them your brightest indirect spot. Rotate the tray a quarter-turn each week so growth stays even. Trim with clean scissors — little and often beats one big haircut.
